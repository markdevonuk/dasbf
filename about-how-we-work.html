About the DASBF

How We Work

Key information about the fund's aims, administration, and how financial assistance is provided.

ℹ

This page contains key information about the DASBF. Please read it carefully before making an application.

Aims & Objectives

What the fund is for

On the 1st January 1990 the Devon Ambulance Staff Benevolent Fund (DASBF) was established with a view to creating, sustaining, and developing a fund of monies from which staff currently employed in the Devon sector or who reside in Devon can benefit in times of:

  • ✓ Genuine financial hardship
  • ✓ Retirement
  • ✓ Bereavement — financial support offered to the family

The aim of the DASBF is to develop this fund into a substantial safety net providing a measure of security for its members. The DASBF continually reviews and examines ways of aiding those staff who suffer genuine financial hardship, and each application will be treated on individual merit at the absolute discretion of the Executive Committee.

Funding

Where the money comes from

The fund sits in a ring-fenced account which receives income from:

  • ✓ Member subscriptions from the DASBF Membership Scheme
  • ✓ Direct voluntary contributions from staff
  • ✓ Donations from the public, funeral collections, and public events

Administration

How we're run

Executive Committee Members are appointed by DASBF members at the AGM and form the Board to administer the fund. A minimum of four committee members must participate in any meeting to form a quorum.

The Executive Committee meets at least twice per calendar year. Local and regional representatives are appointed to assist with administration — including visiting those requesting assistance, helping prepare their case, and answering questions about donations.

All funds are held in a ring-fenced account. Donations made to the fund will be shown in the accounts, but recipients and reasons for donations are kept confidential. Accounts are audited annually.

🔒

Complete Confidentiality

The Executive Committee treats every application for assistance and all other matters with the utmost confidentiality. All data relating to members is held securely and treated as confidential at all times. Your employer is never informed of any application or award.

Grants

How to apply for financial assistance

Applications for financial assistance must be made in writing with full supporting evidence to the Chairman of the Executive Committee. Grants are only considered where there is clear evidence of financial hardship or difficulty — each case is assessed on its own merit.

When considering your application, the committee will want to know whether you have any other possible sources of help available to you, including savings, earnings, household income, credit facilities, and support from immediate family. All circumstances are considered with sensitivity and discretion.

Eligibility

Who can apply for assistance

The fund provides assistance to serving employees of South Western Ambulance Service NHS Trust based or residing anywhere in Devon. Eligibility for financial assistance relates to genuine financial hardship.

  • ✓ A case of genuine need must be identified
  • ✓ A detailed financial report may be required for presentation to the committee
  • ✓ All cases are treated as confidential
  • ✓ The Trustees may at their absolute discretion offer a Hardship Grant
