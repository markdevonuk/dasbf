About the DASBF

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about membership, the lottery, and how to get help.

Membership

Download the membership application form from the Downloads page, print and complete it, and return it to the address shown on the form. You can also contact any committee member directly for help signing up.
Download the change of details form from the Downloads page, complete it, and send it to the address on the form. We will update your details as soon as possible.
Download the leaving form from the Downloads page, complete it, and send it to the address on the form.
You can continue your membership by paying in advance of your anticipated extended unpaid leave. For more information please contact enquiries@dasbf.co.uk stating your intention.

Retirement

Download the retirement claim form from the Downloads page, print and complete it, and return it to the address shown on the form. Please do this in good time before your retirement date.
Unfortunately, you will not be able to rejoin the DASBF after full retirement, even if returning on a bank contract. However, you will still receive your full retirement grant.

Lottery

Quarterly — four times a year, in February, May, August, and November. Results are posted on the DASBF Facebook page after each draw.
You will be contacted by a member of the Executive Committee who will arrange a convenient time and place for the cheque presentation. Payments can also be made by bank transfer — for details contact karen.marriott@swast.nhs.uk.

Hardship Grants

Start by speaking confidentially to your Local Area Representative — they will help you complete the hardship grant application form. You can also download the form directly from the Downloads page and return it to the address shown.
Your application will be reviewed by a minimum of three Executive Committee members. They will discuss your claim and advise you of their decision within 24 hours.

Death in Service

You can nominate a beneficiary by downloading and returning the beneficiary form from the Downloads page. If no instructions are received, the Death in Service payment will go to your next of kin.
